City of Sammamish — Park Volunteer Events Environment
Age: 14+ solo (13 and under w/ adult) When: Various weekdays and weekends, 2–3 hrs Where: Various parks across Sammamish

Help restore local parks by removing invasive plants, adding native plantings, building trails, and restoring wetlands. Frequent one-time events with water, snacks, gloves, and tools provided.

How to sign up

  1. 1Go to the opportunities portal (link below) and create a free Galaxy Digital account
  2. 2Browse events and click "Respond" on one you want to attend
  3. 3Complete the online minor waiver — a direct link is provided on each event page
  4. 4Show up at the listed park location at the listed time

Volunteers 13 and under must have an adult present and directly supervising them. Events need a minimum number of signups to run — check your email the day before for cancellations.

Mercer Island Parks Environment

  1. 1Find an upcoming restoration event on the Mercer Island Parks calendar (link below)
  2. 2Register for the event — some events register through EarthCorps.org, and others are limited-capacity and ask you to sign up via the event page

  4. 4Show up at the listed park — follow the "Volunteer Event" signs

Volunteers 14 and under must have an adult present at a 1:3 ratio. Events are often capacity-limited, so register ahead. Questions: restoration@mercerisland.gov or 206-275-7757.

Washington Trails Association Environment
Age: 14+ When: Weekdays and weekends Where: Eastside trails and beyond

Maintain hiking trails across Washington — clearing brush, improving drainage, and doing the physical work that keeps trails open and safe. WTA provides all tools, training, and crew leaders. No experience needed.

How to sign up

  1. 1Search the work party schedule (link below) by region and date
  2. 2Click "Join Work Party" and fill out a short registration form
  3. 3Print out the annual waiver from the link sent to your email. Both you and a parent sign it and bring the printed copy to the trailhead on the day
  4. 4Show up at the listed trailhead at the listed time

The annual waiver only needs to be printed and signed once per year — your parent doesn't need to attend the event itself.

Bellevue Botanical Garden Environment
Age: 16–17 When: Tue/Thu mornings, Jan–Oct Where: Bellevue (12001 Main St)

Help maintain a 53-acre public garden as a Garden Assistant, or support youth workshops and events. Tools and training provided in one of the most peaceful settings on this list.

How to sign up

  1. 1Read the Parks & Community Services Volunteer Handbook and BBG Volunteer Guide (linked on the page below)
  2. 2Submit an application through the online VicNet portal (linked on the same page)
  3. 3Attend a one-time in-person orientation, required before your first shift — email BBGVolunteer@bellevuewa.gov or call 425-452-6826 to schedule
  4. 4After orientation, email the Volunteer Lead to schedule your first shift, then sign up for future shifts through VicNet

Requires a one-time in-person orientation — geared toward volunteers who'll come back regularly, not a single drop-in visit. Summer gardening applications open May 29.

Hopelink Food Market Food · Community
Age: 16+ When: Mon–Fri, some evenings Where: Bellevue, Kirkland, Shoreline

Help run Hopelink's grocery-style food markets, where families shop for free and choose items that fit their needs. Stock shelves, check in shoppers, and sort donations at locations in Bellevue, Redmond, Kirkland, and Shoreline.

How to sign up

  1. 1Email volunteerwithus@hopelink.org or call 425-869-6066 to fill out the volunteer application
  2. 2Complete a background check (can take up to a week)
  3. 3Once cleared, attend a virtual volunteer orientation (90 min, held 2–4 times per month online)
  4. 4Sign up for shifts at hopelink.volunteerhub.com

Minimum 3-month commitment, about 4 hours per month. Plan for about a week between applying and your first shift while the background check clears.
