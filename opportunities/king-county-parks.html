King County Parks

With King County Parks, you'll join restoration events at parks across the Eastside, doing the hands-on work that keeps natural areas healthy — pulling invasive weeds, planting native trees and shrubs, spreading wood chips along trails, and clearing litter. Events happen regularly throughout the year at a rotating set of locations, and the county provides tools, gloves, and instruction, so you can show up knowing nothing about restoration and leave having learned something. This is a strong choice if you like being outdoors, don't mind getting dirty, and feel good doing physical work that visibly improves a place. The flexibility is a real plus — you pick individual events rather than committing to a schedule. It's less appealing if you dislike being cold or wet (events run rain or shine), or if physical outdoor labor isn't something you enjoy. Under-18s need a printed parent waiver, but no parent needs to attend.

Questions?

  • Portal: parksvolunteer.kingcounty.gov
