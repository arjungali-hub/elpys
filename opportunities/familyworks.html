FamilyWorks

FamilyWorks fights hunger in North Seattle through a neighborhood food bank and monthly community food drives. The most reliable way in as a teen is Supermarket Saturday: once a month, volunteers staff a table outside the Wallingford QFC, handing out flyers and collecting food donations from shoppers — friendly, public-facing work that's open to all ages. The in-building food bank itself (sorting and stocking shelves) is currently waitlist-only, since space is limited; teens 14–15 can work it during school breaks and 16+ on Thursday afternoons, whenever a spot opens. This is a good fit if you're comfortable approaching strangers for a few hours once a month and want a flexible, low-commitment way to help fight food insecurity nearby.
