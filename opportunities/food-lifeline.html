Food Lifeline

At Food Lifeline's Hunger Solution Center in Seattle's South Park neighborhood, you'll work a fast-paced two-hour shift in a large warehouse, sorting and repacking donated food that gets distributed to more than 300 food banks across Western Washington. Expect to be on your feet, moving quickly as part of a team — boxing produce, repacking bulk items, and keeping the line moving. The atmosphere is energetic and surprisingly fun, with music and a real sense of collective momentum as you watch pallets of food come together. Choose this if you thrive on high-energy group work and want to see a big, tangible result in a short time. It's less suited to anyone who prefers slow, quiet, or solo tasks. Everyone needs the signed parent consent form (valid for the year); ages 10–15 also need an adult with them on shift, while 16+ can come fully on their own. The South Park location is about 25–30 minutes from Bellevue.
