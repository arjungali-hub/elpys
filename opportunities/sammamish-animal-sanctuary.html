Sammamish Animal Sanctuary

The Sammamish Animal Sanctuary — actually located in Renton, about twenty minutes from Bellevue — provides a permanent home for rescued barnyard animals like goats, pigs, cows, and chickens. As a volunteer you'll be hands-on with daily animal care: feeding, cleaning enclosures, and helping with the everyday work that keeps the sanctuary running, with training built into your first shift. It's a peaceful, rural setting and a deeply rewarding place for anyone who connects with animals. This is the right choice if you want a genuine, ongoing relationship with the animals and the place, since the sanctuary asks for a minimum three-month commitment — that's the catch to weigh seriously. It's not designed for a one-time visit or an occasional drop-in, so it suits someone ready to show up consistently. You start by filling out an online application, after which you're directed to schedule your first shift. Ages 16 and up can volunteer independently; 14–15 may be considered with prior experience.
