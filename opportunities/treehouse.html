Treehouse Store

The Treehouse Store is a free shopping space in Seattle's Central District where kids and teens in foster care pick out clothes, shoes, books, and supplies — no cost, no stigma, just shopping like anyone else. As a volunteer, you're behind the scenes making that experience possible: sorting and tagging donations, restocking shelves, and prepping the store before shopping days. It's hands-on, organizing-heavy work in a retail-like setting, similar to working a stockroom. This is a great fit if you care about foster youth and like tidy, methodical tasks, and it's especially accessible since one-time shifts are easy to book — ongoing weekly or biweekly roles exist too, but those currently have a waitlist. Volunteers 12–15 need an adult with them; 16 and up can volunteer solo.

Questions?
