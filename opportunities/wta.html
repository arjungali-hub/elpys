Washington Trails Association

The Washington Trails Association runs trail work parties across the region, including Eastside trails, where you'll do the real labor of keeping hiking trails usable — clearing brush, improving drainage, building water bars, and repairing tread. Experienced crew leaders guide every project, and WTA provides the tools and training, so beginners are genuinely welcome. The work is physical and often strenuous, set in beautiful outdoor places, with a strong sense of accomplishment when you look back at a stretch of trail you helped rebuild. This is ideal if you love hiking and the outdoors and want to give back to the trails you use, and if you don't mind a demanding day of work in the woods. It's not the right fit if you're looking for something light, indoor, or close to home, since some trailheads involve a drive. Under-18s need an annual waiver, printed and signed by a parent, who doesn't need to attend the work party itself.

How to sign up

  1. 1Search the work party schedule (link below) by region and date

  3. 3Print out the annual waiver from the link sent to your email. Both you and a parent sign it and bring the printed copy to the trailhead on the day
  4. 4Show up at the listed trailhead at the listed time
