EarthCorps

EarthCorps organizes habitat restoration events, mostly on Saturdays, at parks and green spaces around Puget Sound including several Eastside locations. You'll spend the day removing invasive plants like blackberry and ivy, planting natives, and helping forests and shorelines recover — guided by staff who teach you everything on site, with tools, gloves, and snacks provided. The vibe is communal and mission-driven; you're working in a group toward a visible goal, and there's a satisfying camaraderie to spending a morning pulling ivy alongside other people who care about the environment. Pick this if you're passionate about nature, enjoy group settings, and want work that's physical and outdoors. It's a weekend commitment that eats up a Saturday, so it's less convenient if your weekends are packed, and like all outdoor restoration, it's not for anyone who'd rather stay clean and indoors. Under-18s need a parent to complete an online youth waiver beforehand.
