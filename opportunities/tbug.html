tBUG — The Bellevue Urban Garden

tBUG is a three-acre urban farm tucked into Bellevue, run almost entirely by volunteers and dedicated to growing fresh vegetables for people facing food insecurity in King County. Volunteering here means real farm work — harvesting vegetables, planting seedlings, weeding beds, watering, and helping build and maintain the space. It's hands-in-the-dirt, outdoor, seasonal work with a grassroots, community-garden feel rather than a polished institutional one. You'll enjoy this if you're drawn to growing food, like the meditative side of garden work, and care about where food comes from and who gets to eat it. The flip side is that it's less structured than some other options — you arrange your time by signing up through the Teens Who Care listing or contacting the garden directly — so it suits someone comfortable with a looser, more self-directed setup rather than a rigid schedule.
