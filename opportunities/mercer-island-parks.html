Mercer Island Parks

Mercer Island Parks runs restoration events across the island's parks and forested areas, where you'll remove invasive ivy and blackberry, plant native species, spread mulch, and do light trail work. Events happen every other Saturday plus some alternating weekdays, with tools, gloves, and snacks provided — no experience needed. This is a good match if you like outdoor, physical work and want a flexible, drop-in-style commitment rather than a recurring weekly shift. Because events are often capacity-limited, it rewards someone willing to check the calendar regularly and register early. Volunteers 14 and under need an adult with them at a 1:3 ratio, while 15 and up can sign up and attend solo.

How to sign up

  1. 1Find an upcoming restoration event on the Mercer Island Parks calendar (link below)
  2. 2Register for the event — some events register through EarthCorps.org, and others are limited-capacity and ask you to sign up via the event page
  3. 3If you're under 18, have a parent sign the minor consent form (linked on the volunteer page) and bring it
  4. 4Show up at the listed park — follow the "Volunteer Event" signs
